User Research

I sent a mass online survey to a subReddit forum and social media to better understand my target audience. From the research gathered, about more than half (55%) still go to comic book stores to purchase physical comics and merchandise. The majoirty (78%) of respondents have used comic-related apps but more than half (57.5%) still prefer to go to physical stores to purchase comics.

In addition to the mass survey sent to slack channels and a subreddit for comics, an in-person interview was conducted to better understand one of my later identified personas: the rusher. Valuable features were noted during the interview. Some examples include finding comics a store had in stock without the hassle of asking in person and the value of reading comics in person versus on an app.

Competitive Analysis

My main competition was confirmed by my mass online survey. I discovered from the survey that 88.5% of respondents have used apps that involve comics (both buying and reading online) such as Amazon, DC, and Marvel. 57.7% of respondents found ease of use and reliability to be the best part of their apps of choice while 26.2% complained that navigation problems were the main issue.

A SWOT Analysis was conducted to better understand my competition from a big picture perspective. This helped me gain insight into how ComicCrusade would be positioned next to the identified competitors.

User Stories

After making personas collecting the survey results, user stories were made using Google Docs to help capture the potential needs of the users.

User Flows

User flows were created using the porgram draw.io. They were based off of the main user stories created in the beginning of the project.

Wireframes

I made wireframes based off of the main user flows first drafting them as sketches using my sketchbook and pencil. Afterwards, they were scanned and replicated using Adobe Illustrator.

User Testing

Testing was performed using a combination of InVision and UsabilityHub. Throughout the project, all wireframes (from lo-fidelity to final mockups) were tested and later updated based on the feedback provided from peers via Slack channels.

Prototype

I used InVision to create a clickable prototype for surveying and testing potential users for feedback.

Conclusion

I came out of this project with a real appreciation for Google's Material Design Systems as well as the overall process of crafting mobile designs for apps. Sketching helped me immensely when confronted with creative block. As they always say, the pen is mightier than the sword~
