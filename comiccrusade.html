    User Research

    I sent a mass online survey to a subReddit forum; slack channels; and social media to better understand my target audience.

    55% still visit comic bookstores to purchase physical comics and merchandise.

    52% would consider using an app to help them find the comics they are looking for.

    56.3% want to continue experiencing the pleasure of owning a physical comic over a digital copy.

    43.7% want to engage in face-to-face interactions with the staff and other customers at the stores.

    In addition to the mass survey sent, I conducted an in-person interview to better understand one of my later identified personas: the rusher. The interviewee mentioned valuable information for furthering the project's development. Some examples include finding comics in nearby stores and the value of reading physical comics versus electronic ones.

    Personas

    I crafted personas off of the survey results and in-person interview. Both helped me identify my target groups.

    Alice

    23-Year-Old Designer

    MOTIVATIONS

    Alice casually reads comics in her free time. She normally buys comics at a store but has been buying them and other books online. This is due to the cheaper prices and being in stock. If the comic store near her workplace had what she wanted at a decent price, she would buy her physical copies there.

    “I typically buy comics and other books online since buying them at a store can be costly and they sometimes don't have what I'm looking for.”

    GOALS

    • Buy comics at a physical store if in stock and cheaper.
    • Partake in comic-related events when possible.
    • Connect with other people at a comic store and at comic-related events when possible.

    FRUSTRATIONS

    • Stores don't have what she wants.
    • Price of store comics is too expensive.
    • Would like an app that can help her find the comics she wants when his favorite store is out.

    Zachary

    22-Year-Old Student

    MOTIVATIONS

    Zachary enjoys going to his favorite comic bookstore just a few miles away from his school. He visits the store weekly to visit staff and find new comics. Despite this, he finds it frustrating when new comics he wants aren't in stock.

    "I've been reading comics ever since I was in my teens. I want to keep collecting and reading my favorite comics with the people I interact with.”

    GOALS

    • Visit favorite comic bookstore again every week.
    • Continue buying comics in phsycial format at the store.
    • Continue connecting with others through comic-related events and at the store.

    FRUSTRATIONS

    • Stores don’t always have what he wants.
    • Price of buying physical comics is expensive at stores.
    • Would like an app that can help him find the comics he wants when his favorite store is out.

    Competitive Analysis

    I made a SWOT analysis to better understand my competition (Amazon, Marvel, and DC) from a big picture perspective. This gave me insight into ComicCrusade's positioning.

    Strengths

    • Offer wide variety of comics at a low cost.
    • Large network with customers.
    • Fast and responsive.

    Weaknesses

    • High over-head costs.
    • Customers choosing physical copies over e-books.

    Opportunities

    • Possible sales boosts with kindle readers.
    • Android OS expansion.
    • New business deals in UK and Southeast Asia.

    Threats

    • Cheap price model.
    • Growing expansion from competitors on mobile devices.
    • Data breaches.

    My mass online survey confirmed my main competition. 88.5% of respondents have used apps that involve comics (both buying and reading online) such as Amazon, DC, and Marvel. 57.7% of respondents found an ease of use and reliability to be the best part of their apps of choice. 26.2% complained that navigation problems were the main issue.

    User Testing

    I conducted user testing using InVision, UsabilityHub, and Google Forms. All testing and results are displayed below.

    Challenge #1 - Finding Stores

    Users tested ComicCrusade's first primary function by opening up the app and interact with the search bar.

    Lo-Fi Store Locator

    Lo-Fi Store Results

    Lo-Fi Store Details

    From the research gathered, most of the users found a store easily. The only confusing part was what to click on the results screen since all of them said the same thing. As a result, I made changes to ease the use of finding a store. Such changes include reducing the number of stores shown on screen; giving names to available stores nearby; adding the logo along with a circle icon to show the store selected on the map screen and adding more details for the store screens once a store has been selected.

    Conclusion

    I came out of this project appreciating Google's Material Design Systems. In addition, the overall process of crafting mobile designs for apps. The comic industry is huge. From Marvel to DC to even indie comics, many people love a good visual story. ComicCrusade aimed to help unify comic book fans and those interested in comics.

    Lessons Learned

    When in doubt, sketch! Sketching helped me immensely when confronted with creative block. It pays to write your ideas out so you can sort through them and make a decision easier. Multiple times I've had to backpedal and revise my wireframes using sketching. When one frame was just not working, I had to conjure a new look for it to work better.

    It's better to have a finished "good enough" project than an unfinished "perfect" one. You can spend an eternity trying to make a "perfect" project. You have to learn when to draw the line and say, "Enough is enough. If this keeps up, I'll never be done." No design is perfect and you have to go with your gut when you would consider marking something as done.

    Always remember your main innovation. It's easy to get lost in the weeds with trying to handle other main aspects of the project. In addition, forgetting what your project is about regarding its main differentiator(s). Backpedalling is necessary for remembering the main objective frequently.
