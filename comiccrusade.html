    • ComicCrusade

    6 weeks | Android OS App

    Android app dedicated to finding the comics you’re looking for in nearby stores.

    User Research

    I sent a mass online survey to a subReddit forum; slack channels; and social media to better understand my target audience. From the research gathered, here's what was discovered:

    55% still go to comic book stores to purchase physical comics and merchandise.

    52% would consider using an app to help them find the comcis they are looking for.

    56.3% want to continue experiencing the pleasure of owning a physical comic over a digital one.

    43.7% want to engage in face-to-face interactions with the staff and other customers at the stores.

    In addition to the mass survey sent, an in-person interview was conducted to better understand one of my later identified personas: the rusher. Valuable features were noted during the interview. Some examples include finding comics a store had in stock without the hassle of asking in person and the value of reading comics in person versus on an app.

    Personas

    Personas were crafted based off of the results of the research conducted in this project. Both the survey results and the 1-on-1 interview I performed helped me identify my target groups:

    Alice

    23-Year-Old Designer

    MOTIVATIONS

    Alice casually reads comics when she has free time from work. She normally buys comics at a store but has been buying them and other books online due to cheaper prices and always having them in stock. If the comic store near her workplace had what she wanted at a decent price, she would buy her physical copies there.

    “I typically buy comics and other books online since buying them at a store can be costly and they sometimes don’t have what I’m looking for.”

    GOALS

    • Buy comics at physical store if in stock and cheaper.
    • Partake in comic-related events when possible.
    • Connect with other people at comic store and at comic-related events when possible.

    FRUSTRATIONS

    • Stores don’t have what he wants in stock when visiting sometimes.
    • Price of buying physical comics is higher than buying and reading it online.
    • Would like an app that can help him find the comics she wants when his favorite store is out.

    Zachary

    22-Year-Old Student

    MOTIVATIONS

    Zachary enjoys going to his favorite comic book store just a few miles away from his school. He visits there weekly to see what’s hot on the shelves along with interacting with the staff he knows well. Despite the positive nature of the store, he gets frustrated when the new comics he wants aren’t in stock.

    “I’ve been reading comics ever since I was in my teens. I want to keep collecting and reading my favorite comics with the good people I interact with.”

    GOALS

    • Visit his favorite comic book store again every week.
    • Continue buying comics in phsycial format at his store.
    • Continue connecting with others through comic-related events and at his favorite comic store.

    FRUSTRATIONS

    • Stores don’t have what he wants in stock when visiting sometimes.
    • Price of buying physical comics is higher than buying and reading it online.
    • Would like an app that can help him find the comics he wants when his favorite store is out.

    Competitive Analysis

    A SWOT Analysis was conducted to better understand my competition (Amazon, Marvel, and DC) from a big picture perspective . This helped me gain insight into how ComicCrusade would be positioned next to the identified competitors.

    Strengths

    • Offer wide variety of comics at a low cost.
    • Large network with customers.
    • Fast and responsive.

    Weaknesses

    • High over-head costs.
    • Customers choosing physical copies over e-books.

    Opportunities

    • Possible sales boosts with kindle readers.
    • Android OS expansion.
    • New business deals in UK and Southeast Asia.

    Threats

    • Cheap price model.
    • Growing expansion from competitors on mobile devices.
    • Data breaches.

    My main competition was confirmed by my mass online survey. I discovered from the survey that 88.5% of respondents have used apps that involve comics (both buying and reading online) such as Amazon, DC, and Marvel. 57.7% of respondents found ease of use and reliability to be the best part of their apps of choice while 26.2% complained that navigation problems were the main issue.

    User Testing

    Testing was performed using InVision, UsabilityHub, and Google Forms. Here's what was tested:

    Challenge #1 - Finding Stores

    ComicCrusade's first primary function was tested in order to understand how users would respond to its functionality.

    Lo-Fi Store Locator

    Lo-Fi Store Results

    Lo-Fi Store Details

    From the research gathered, most of the users found finding a store easy. The only confusing part was what to click on the results screen since all of them said the same thing. As a result, changes were made to ease the use of finding a store. Such changes include reducing the amount of stores shown on screen; giving names to available stores nearby; adding the logo along with a circle icon to show the store selected; and adding more details for the store screens once a store has been selected.

    Conclusion

    I came out of this project with a real appreciation for Google's Material Design Systems as well as the overall process of crafting mobile designs for apps. The world of comics is a massive one. From Marvel to DC to even indie comics, a lot of people love a good visual story. ComicCrusade aimed to help unify comic book fans and those wanting to get into comics.

    Lessons Learned

    When in doubt, sketch! Sketching helped me immensely when confronted with creative block. It pays to write your ideas out so you can sort through them and make a decision easier. Multiple times I've had to go back and revise my wireframes using sketching. When one frame was just not working, I had to come up with a new look for it to work better.

    It's better to have a finished "good enough" project than an unfinished "perfect" project. You can spend an eternity trying to make what you would consider a "prefect" project. You have to learn when to draw the line and say, "Enough is enough. If I keep this up, I'll never be finished." No design is perfect and you have to go with your gut when you would consider marking something as done.

    Always remember your main innovation. It’s really easy to get lost in the weeds with trying to handle other main aspects of the project and forget what your project is all about regarding its main differentiator(s). That’s why it's imperative to go back and remeber the main objective frequently.
