    • FoodSwitch

    6 weeks | Product Showcase App

    A web-based app for people who want to live a healthier lifestyle.

    VIEW PROTOTYPE

    Meta-Challenge: apply knowledge of designing landing pages and dashboards.

    Underlying Task: create a landing page and dashboard design for a diet-tracking app.

    My Roles: digital product research and screen design.

    User Research

    I sent a mass online survey to social media and slack channels to better understand my potential users. From there, here's what I received:

    69% want a more straightforward way of tracking their diet.

    85.7% of respondents have considered finding ways to improve their current lifestyle.

    71% of respondents have used a diet tracking app before (i.e., MyFitnessPal; Fooducate; MyPlate).

    The survey results revealed that most of the users try to live a healthier lifestyle but struggle with it due to their current diet and health habits. Users who have used a diet tracking app in the past loved tracking their diets but often complained about the mundanity and complexity of inputting their calories and exercise. They had to go through too many entries to input one food item or meal.

    Personas

    Personas were crafted based off of the results of the research conducted in this project. Both the survey results and the 1-on-1 interview I performed helped me identify my target groups:

    Eric

    23-Year-Old Designer

    MOTIVATIONS

    Eric has often struggled with living healthier due to working long hours. In addition, having an addiction to fast food. Despite this, he wishes to live a healthier lifestyle than the one he currently has. He has made small adjustments to his diet by eating more fruits and going to a fast food restaurant only for lunch instead of both lunch and dinner. Eric loves to track his progress on his health improvements by writing down his caloric intake and what kind of foods he eats but finds the process mundane and tedious. He wishes for a tool that can help him with making the process more enjoyable and rewarding.

    “I’ve always struggled eating healither and being fit. I’m determined to take it up to the next level.”

    GOALS

    • Buy comics at physical store if in stock and cheaper.
    • Partake in comic-related events when possible.
    • Connect with other people at comic store and at comic-related events when possible.

    FRUSTRATIONS

    • Keeping track of calories consumed and quality of food eaten.
    • Reducing number of visits to fast food places.
    • Would like to find software that can help him.

    Matthew

    28-Year-Old Student

    MOTIVATIONS

    Matthew follows a popular diet known as the Paleo diet—a diet our ancestors, the Neanderthals, followed. While he enjoys this, he doesn’t get much exercise. He tries pushing himself to do more but can’t find something to keep him motivated. He also wishes for an app that can help in tracking caloric intake and exercise amount better than his current app. While the one he uses does work, he complains about how missing a day can create data inconsistencies with diet tracking even when he usually ate like any other day. Matthew hopes he can find what he is looking for so he never has to worry about missing a day of logging due to forgetting or something urgent comes up.

    “I want an app that can make tracking my diet, the Paleo diet, and exercise much more enjoyable.”

    GOALS

    • Increasing amount of exercise done per week.
    • Finding an easier way to track his current diet.
    • Would like to find a better app than the one he uses currently.

    FRUSTRATIONS

    • Current app is making diet tracking too frustrating.
    • Trying to find time to exercise and track it.
    • Having to constantly remind himself not to miss a day of tracking his diet.

    Competitive Analysis

    A few competitors were identified in the making of this project: Myfitnesspal, Fooducate, MyPlate. A SWOT Analysis was conducted to understand better my competition and FoodSwitch's positioning. Here's what I gathered:

    Strengths

    • Large database of food items to access.
    • Well known in the marketplace.
    • Reliable and easy to pull up.
    • Large community support.

    Weaknesses

    • High over-head costs.
    • Investment issues with stockholders.

    Opportunities

    • Possible sales boosts with kindle readers.
    • Android OS expansion.
    • New business deals in UK and Southeast Asia.

    Threats

    • Cheap price model.
    • Growing expansion from competitors on mobile devices.
    • Data breaches.

    My mass online survey confirms my main competition. The results showed that 71% of surveyors have interacted with these apps before.

    User Testing

    I performed user testing by asking my design peers for feedback on usability via an InVision prototype. Throughout the project, all wireframes (from lo-fidelity to final mockups) were tested and later updated based on the input provided from peers via Slack channels. Here's what happened:

    Challenge #1 - Clearer Instructions for Switch Page

    At the beginning of the user testing, my lo-fidelity prototype was created and sent to peers for testing. The main screen to be tested was the main innovation of the app itself: the Switch page.

    Lo-Fi Store Locator

    Lo-Fi Store Results

    From the research gathered, most of the users found it confusing and frustrating on how the screen should work. As a result, changes were made to ease the use of using the screen. Such changes include simplifying the usability of the screen via the buttons in the middle; adding a drop-down menu for all items to include multiple options for replacement foods; and immediately updating the graph upon selecting a chosen food.

    Challenge #2 - Remodeling the Dashboard

    The dashboard for FoodSwitch was tested to see how users would respond from using its functions.

    Lo-Fi Dashboard 1

    Lo-Fi Dashboard 2

    From testing, most users found it confusing to use and where overwhelmed by the multiple icons and buttons on the screen. Therefore, I performed a remodeling of the dashboard. I cleaned the interface, brought both the dashboard and Switch graph to the same screen, and made it easier for users to read their overall health status.

    Conclusion

    This project gave me significant insight into how marketing a product or service works. From innovative features to pricing; testimonials; and more. Such knowledge will serve me well in the future.

    Lessons Learned

    It’s about the client’s clients, not me or the client. The customer is the one who’s going to interact with the product/service. By tailoring it to their needs and expectations, you’ll bring in more business. In testing, you’ll discover exciting trends and mindsets of users you never could’ve imagined (ex: What if I want to forecast foods with suggestions instead of a static list of healthy foods?). This feedback alone will make a better product.

    You are going to jump back and forth quite a bit during project progression. I did a lot of hopping with managing the aspects of the project. From the user testing to the visual design, development, prototyping, etc. There is no formulaic way of conducting a UX project like this. Even so, it did grant me fulfillment. I was able to do something different almost every day which eliminated mundanity of the job.

    Feedback is the key to building a better product.This is the truth to everything regarding projects. Feedback helped me find all kinds of errors, flaws, bugs, and glitches that I could not have seen by myself. Like a gem, a project must be polished until it gleams with purity. Only then will it show its real value to others.
