My Journey

Building my career as a UX Designer has been an adventure full of twists and turns. Many things influenced my ultimate decision of becoming one: starting my cartooning hobby, pursuing a technical degree in college, and overcoming an identity crisis post-graduation by the Summer of 2016.

My journey began six years ago when I first took up cartooning as a hobby. I was inspired by a few of my classmates and an online artist by the name of SailorEnergy who drew my favorite cartoon/anime characters in amazing poses and scenes. As a result, I started drawing cartoons mid-January of 2012 by grabbing some plain print paper and a #2 pencil.

In college, I pursued a degree in Management Information Systems (MIS) due to my exposure of technology since the age of seven. My major classes and group projects exposed me to subjects such as UI design; Information Architecture; Graphics Design; and Programming (Visual Basic and C#). These experiences would later play a huge part in my pursuit of becoming a UX Designer.

In the Summer of 2016, I had no job in my major field of study lined up and was confused about what to do next. Reflecting on my accumulated experiences, I decided to pursue a career in UX Design based on my pendulum passions (art and technology) and what I. By December 2016, I applied for taking the designer track at Bloc––an online bootcamp--and got started on my studies.

My overall journey was like being an adventurer in a fictional tale. I had to overcome many obstacles; mistakes; and errors that made me stronger and better overall both in life and as a designer. The journey is never ending but enjoyable every step of the way.
